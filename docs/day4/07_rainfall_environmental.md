# Rainfall and Environmental Analysis

Water balance and hydrological modeling require compiling and summarizing spatial rainfall grids across catchment areas.

---

## 1. Rainfall Grids and Zonal Statistics

* **Rainfall Grids:** Gridded datasets (e.g., CHIRPS daily rainfall or interpolated rain gauge surfaces) provide spatial rainfall distributions.

* **Sub-Catchment Summarization:** By overlaying sub-catchment boundaries on top of rainfall grids, hydrologists can run **Zonal Statistics** to calculate the average rainfall volume entering each basin.

---

## 2. Environmental Indicators

* **Hydrological Connectivity Indices:** Model how pollutants or sediment run off from farm fields, travel through small streams, and accumulate in larger rivers.

* **Riparian Buffer Zone Health:** Analyzing land cover within 100 to 500 meters of river channels to assess riparian health and buffer efficiency.
